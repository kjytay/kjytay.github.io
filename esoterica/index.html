Esoterica

Inspired by Christian Lawson-Perfect's Interesting Esoterica page, this is a page of references that I have found interesting over the years. Some of them talk about practical matters; others don't. I just find them stimulating enough to keep them on hand. May some of these references spark joy (or ideas!) in your own life!

Math

  • N. Pottekkat, 2026.The Fastest Way to Board an Airplane.Love the simulations!
  • J. Propp, 2025. Estimating π with a Coin.Simple method and simple proof!
  • J. K. Tay, 2022. Conway’s fruitful fractions (or how to generate all the prime numbers by multiplying one of 14 fractions). Really interesting how he encodes a computer program that generates all the prime numbers with multiplication of fractions.
  • C. L. Adler, and J. Tanton, 2018. "π is the Minimum Value for Pi."
  • D. Kozen, and A. Silva, 2013. "On Moessner's Theorem." How a simple set of rules unexpectedly leads to generating the sequence of nth powers.
  • G. Galperin, 2003. "Playing Pool With π (The Number π From a Billard Point of View)." A method for estimating digits of pi with billard balls. My description here.

Statistics/Probability

  • L. Albert, 2020. "a soccer win probability model." Example of using a Poisson process.
  • T. Reddad, 2019. "A uniform sum threshold problem." A nice write-up on the expected number of U[0,1] draws needed for the sum to exceed 1.
  • A. Downey, 2015. "The Inspection Paradox is Everywhere." A blog post with several examples of the inspection paradox.

Counter-examples

  • A J-convex (midpoint-convex) function which is not convex

AI/Engineering

  • ud2.rip, 2026.Top 20 worldwide with social-engineering and a cheat that's still undetected. Really interesting read on how they built a cheat that works on VSRGs, intersection between RL and social engineering.
  • S. Rose, 2025. Prompt caching: 10x cheaper LLM tokens, but how? Really intuitive visual explanation of how LLMs work. A lot of other beautiful visual essays here.
  • C. Olah, 2015. Understanding LSTM Networks. Intuitive explanation of how LSTMs work.

Essays

  • R. Sutton, The Bitter Lesson: A short essay on the fact that in AI research, general methods that leverage computation are ultimately the most effective by a large margin.
  • R. Hamming, You and your research
  • A. Karpathy, A survival guide to a PhD
  • M. Schonlau, How to finish a Ph.D.
  • T. Khovanova, How to Read a Math Paper.
